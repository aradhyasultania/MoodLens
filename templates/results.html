ü´Å Breathing Exercise

What is it?

A 3-minute guided breathing exercise designed to help you feel more centered and calm. It's a simple yet powerful technique that can be done anywhere, anytime.

How to do it:

1. Sit comfortably and close your eyes. Make sure your back is straight and you're in a quiet environment.

2. Breathe in slowly for 4 counts (inhale).

3. Hold your breath for 4 counts (hold).

4. Breathe out slowly for 6 counts (exhale).

5. Repeat this cycle 3 times.

6. Notice how you feel after the exercise.

This exercise helps activate your parasympathetic nervous system and promotes calm.

üåç Grounding Technique

What is it?

A 5-4-3-2-1 grounding exercise that helps you feel present and safe in moments of anxiety or overwhelm.

How to do it:

1. Name 5 things you can see around you.

2. Name 4 things you can touch.

3. Name 3 things you can hear.

4. Name 2 things you can smell.

5. Name 1 thing you can taste.

6. Take a deep breath and notice you're here.

This technique helps ground you in the present moment and reduce anxiety.

üßò Body Scan

What is it?

A gentle body awareness exercise that helps you release tension and connect with your body.

How to do it:

1. Start at the top of your head and notice any tension or sensations.

2. Move down to your shoulders and notice any tightness.

3. Check your chest and breathing.

4. Notice your stomach area and any discomfort.

5. Feel your legs and feet.

6. Take a deep breath.

This helps you connect with your body and release tension.

üìù Journaling

What is it?

Guided writing prompts to help you process and understand your emotions.

How to do it:

Try writing about:

  • What am I feeling right now?
  • What might be causing these feelings?
  • What do I need right now?
  • What am I grateful for today?
  • What would I tell a friend in this situation?

Writing helps process emotions and gain clarity.

üéì Understanding Emotions

What is it?

Learn about different emotions and how to work with them effectively.

Key Points:

  • All emotions are valid and serve a purpose
  • Emotions are temporary and will pass
  • It's okay to feel multiple emotions at once
  • Emotions provide information about your needs
  • You can learn to work with difficult emotions
  • Seeking help is a sign of strength
